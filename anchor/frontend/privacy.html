Privacy & data handling

Honest about what we collect, where it lives, and what's missing.

This is a hackathon prototype. It does not meet UK clinical-grade data-protection requirements. Do not use with real patient data. This page exists so the shape of production-grade compliance is explicit from day one.

What data exists in this prototype

  • Conversation transcripts. Every spoken or typed interaction with Anchor is logged to data/conversation_log.json, including the full LLM response and whether a safety rail rejected it.
  • Carer notifications. Escalation events (distress keywords, falls, missed medication, wandering) in data/carer_notifications.json with timestamp, urgency, and the patient utterance that triggered them.
  • Medication log. Timestamps of confirmed doses.
  • Mood / sleep / behaviour entries. Carer-entered free-text notes.
  • Location. Foreground GPS coordinates (lat, lng, accuracy) while the patient device is on and the browser tab is visible. Last 120 readings only; old points expire.
  • Family photos and voice recordings. Optional uploads in data/family_photos/ and data/family_voices/.
  • Patient profile. Identity, family, routines, preferences, scheduled events, care notes — seeded, and edited by the carer via the schedule editor.

UK GDPR — Article 9 special category

Most of the above is special-category health data under UK GDPR Article 9. Any production deployment would require, before a single patient is onboarded:

  • An Article 9(2) lawful basis — explicit written consent from the patient (while capacity is present) and any Lasting Power of Attorney.
  • A Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) — ICO template, signed off by a named DPO.
  • ICO registration as a data controller.
  • Data Processing Agreements with every sub-processor — in this prototype's architecture that means Anthropic, Z.AI, ElevenLabs, and the hosting provider. Non-trivial: some LLM providers' default terms use prompts for training.
  • Encryption at rest and in transit — TLS is on by default on Render, but the at-rest encryption of the data/*.json files is not implemented in this prototype.
  • A retention policy and deletion mechanism — the prototype has /api/reset but no patient-initiated data export or right-to-erasure workflow.
  • A clear audit trail — who accessed what, when. The prototype has an activity feed for the carer, not an auditable access log.

Capacity and consent

A patient with dementia may lack capacity to consent to data processing at any given moment, even when they had it earlier. Production-grade Anchor would need a capacity-assessment workflow and a Lasting Power of Attorney (LPA) on record. For a non-instantaneous decision — "do we tell the GP about this month's mood trend?" — the Best Interests framework from the Mental Capacity Act 2005 applies.

None of this is implemented here. The prototype assumes Priya has already had these conversations off-platform.

What the prototype does protect

  • No third-party analytics, ads, or tracking pixels. Zero cookies beyond whatever Render's platform injects.
  • No patient data is sent to any service that isn't strictly required to produce a response (LLM provider, TTS provider).
  • The two-layer safety rail blocks ungrounded LLM output before it reaches the patient — a privacy-by-design choice against hallucinated facts about family or health.
  • .env and data/ are in .gitignore; keys and patient state never go into git.

Not a medical device

Anchor is a companion, not a medical device. It does not give medical advice — every attempt is deflected to the GP or Priya. It does not diagnose, triage, or adjust treatment. Fall detection is limited to verbal self-report. Geofence alerts are best-effort foreground-only tracking, not a replacement for in-person supervision.

A production deployment with any diagnostic claim would require MHRA classification as medical-device software (likely Class IIa) with the associated clinical validation burden.
